Daily Nutrition

When eating a strictly vegan diet, it is important to be conscious of the nutrients you would normally get from meat and dairy and incorporate them into your diet through other foods. One of these is protein. To reach your daily quota of protein, you can replace meat-based entrees with beans, tofu, quinoa, seitan, or tempeh, to name a few. Additionally, to avoid a vitamin B12 deficiency, you should try to consume soy products on a regular basis, or take a vitamin B supplement. B vitamins are important in the body's absorption of iron, another nutrient that meat is rich in. Luckily, iron can also be found in dark, leafy greens, legumes, and dried fruits, such as prunes, so you can still get plenty of it while maintaining a vegan diet. Without dairy, you should also make an effort to sustain a healthy level of calcium. Some alternate sources of calcium include fortified soymilk, fortified cereals, and greens such as spinach, kale, turnips, and collard greens.
